Animation Project

I created this 15-second animated video using Adobe Animate to visually explain the process of photosynthesis in an engaging and easy-to-understand way. The animation illustrates key stages, starting with the absorption of sunlight by chlorophyll, the intake of carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, and the release of oxygen as a byproduct. I used smooth motion tweens and carefully labeled visuals to ensure each concept was clearly presented, while transitions between scenes provided a seamless flow.

To make the animation visually appealing, I designed a subtle yellow gradient background to evoke warmth and light, complementing the theme of sunlight in photosynthesis. I added fade-in and fade-out effects for a polished look and incorporated text overlays, such as "Sunlight Absorbed" and "Oxygen Released," to emphasize important steps in the process. This combination of visuals and text ensured the scientific concepts were both informative and visually engaging.

Creating this project presented its own set of challenges. I spent time learning the various features of Adobe Animate, including how to manage registration points for smooth motion and maintain accuracy in depicting biological processes. Despite these hurdles, the experience was incredibly rewarding. The final video effectively communicates the complex process of photosynthesis in a concise format.
